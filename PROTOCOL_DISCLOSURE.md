# Pre-Registration Protocol Disclosure

**Paper.** The Dark Subspace of Fine-Tuning Memorisation
**Date drafted.** 2026-04-28
**Submission target.** ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop (May 8 AOE)

---

## Purpose

This document inventories the pre-registered methodological choices, validity gates, and decision criteria for the paper. Items here were committed to the working repository before the experimental results that depend on them were generated. Each amendment below is dated.

Each numerical claim in the paper is generated by a script in `scripts/dark_subspace/` and recorded as a JSON file under `results/dark_subspace/`.

## Pre-registered items

### PR-1. Membership Inference Setup

- **Members and non-members.** Defined by membership in the controlled fine-tuning corpus split. Member set: `data/memcirc_ctrl_ft/member.jsonl`. Non-member set: `data/memcirc_ctrl_ft/nonmember.jsonl`. 1000 each. (The corpus directory is not bundled; regenerate via `scripts/dark_subspace/build_disjoint_owt_corpus.py` against an OpenWebText source.)
- **Probe.** Linear probe (logistic regression with L2 regularisation, $C{=}1.0$) trained on the leading knowledge direction $d_K$ from the channel decomposition.
- **Score.** Projection onto $d_K$, AUROC over member-vs-nonmember scores.
- **Layer.** Per-model SAE analysis layer chosen before the dark-subspace experiment. Pythia-6.9B: layer 16. Pythia-12B: layer 18. GPT-Neo-2.7B: layer 16. Qwen2-7B: layer 16. Other per-model layers in Appendix Table `tab:model_details` of the manuscript and in `scripts/dark_subspace/configs/oc_roster.json`. Selection rule: layer-wise logistic membership detector on raw mean-pooled residual stream activations under a fixed five-fold split, with the layer of highest cross-validated AUROC selected before any SAE was trained.
- **Reproducibility.** All probe and channel-decomposition scripts under `scripts/dark_subspace/` use deterministic Python/NumPy/torch RNG seeds (default 42). CUDA bit-reproducibility is documented separately in the README ("Reproducibility caveat").

### PR-2. SAE Validity Gates

Gates pre-registered before the multi-seed experiments. Failure of any gate is documented and the model is excluded from the affected analysis.

- **Reconstruction cosine gate.** ~~Mean reconstruction cosine $\geq 0.95$ on the member and non-member corpus.~~ Amended on 2026-05-02 to a tiered gate hierarchy (see Amendment Log). Current operative rule: STRICT gate at reconstruction cosine $\geq 0.90$; YELLOW permissive band at $[0.85, 0.90)$ admitted with disclosure for the directional sign-test cohort observation only; below-permissive admitted only for the directional cohort observation when the comparison does not require high reconstruction cosine, and excluded from any quantitative claim that does. The bundled `results/dark_subspace/paper_claims/sae_quality_exclusions.json` records the per-row recon cosine for Mistral-7B (0.7005) and Llama-3-8B (-0.0334) which fall below the permissive band.
- **L0 sparsity gate.** Implicit: dead-feature fraction $\leq 0.30$.
- **Multi-seed cohort.** Pythia-6.9B is the multi-seed causal anchor with ~~N=6 seeds (42_postfix, 43, 44, 45, 46, 47)~~ N=5 seeds (42_postfix, 43, 44, 45, 46) per Amendment 2026-05-06 (harmonisation; see Amendment Log). The full N=6 cluster is preserved at `results/dark_subspace/paper_claims/p69_n6_complete.json` alongside the N=5 cluster at `p69_n5_harmonized_2026-05-06.json`. Per-metric materiality between the two framings is NEGLIGIBLE; the verifier asserts the materiality verdict directly.

### PR-3. Causal Ablation (K-PC) Decision Criteria

- **Targets.** $K \in \{10, 50, 200\}$ across the four gate-passing models. $K=5$ added later as a cheap robustness extension on the same script.
- **Controls.** C1 random rotation (100 seeds), C2 matched Gaussian noise (100 seeds), C4 random column mask falsifier (20 seeds). All committed to the repository before the primary $K=10$ result was computed.
- **Decisive criterion.** Primary ablation must beat C4 (random column mask) by $\geq 3\sigma$ (approximately 0.06 AUROC at the noise level we observed).
- **Disclosure rule.** If the decisive criterion is not met, the result is reported as subspace-level rather than direction-level, with the C4 magnitude disclosed in the main text.

### PR-4. Cross-Model Decisive Gate Outcome

- Pre-registered: 6 cells (Pythia-6.9B and Qwen2-7B at $K \in \{10, 50, 200\}$) tested against C4.
- Outcome: 0 of 6 cells passed the decisive criterion. Disclosed in the main text and in the limitations.

### PR-5. Bag-of-Words Vocabulary Confound Control

- Bag-of-words classifier trained on the same member/nonmember split, no model or hidden state involvement.
- Pre-registered as a confound control.
- Outcome: AUROC 0.4566 (below chance), rules out token-level vocabulary explanation.
- Source: `results/dark_subspace/generated/bow_ceiling/memcirc_ctrl_ft/results.json`.

### PR-6. Paraphrase Sensitivity Diagnostic

- Word-order-shuffled paraphrase mode.
- Pre-registered as a methodological audit, not a positive robustness claim.
- Outcome: orientation flip on all three audited models (Pythia-6.9B, Qwen2-7B, Pythia-12B). Reported as a methodological caution about bidirectional AUROC reporting.
- Source: `results/dark_subspace/generated/paraphrase_sensitivity/{p69,qwen2,p12b}/results.json`.

### PR-7. Negative Controls Added After the Main Pre-Registration

These controls were added after the main pre-registration but before the corresponding experiments produced results.

- **Pre-fine-tuning baseline.** Channel decomposition probe on un-fine-tuned Pythia-6.9B at layers 12, 14, 16, 18, 20. Output: `results/dark_subspace/generated/behavioral_channels/p69_BASE_pre_ft/`. Rules out "is this a base-model property?"
- **Layer sensitivity sweep.** Channel decomposition probe at layers 12, 14, 16, 18, 20 on the fine-tuned Pythia-6.9B model. Output: `results/dark_subspace/generated/behavioral_channels/p69_epoch5_layer_sweep/`. Rules out "is layer 16 cherry-picked?"
- **K=5 PC ablation.** Same script as $K \in \{10, 50, 200\}$, with $K=5$. Output: `results/dark_subspace/generated/causal_ablation_K5/`. Tightens the K-sweep claim from below.
- **Random-direction baseline.** Sample 100 random unit directions per model, compute member-vs-nonmember AUROC along each, compare distribution to the residual probe AUROC. Rules out "any direction in activation space works."
- **Random-init SAE baseline.** Run dark-subspace evaluation against an untrained random-init SAE of matching shape on Pythia-6.9B. Rules out "any dictionary-shaped operator works."
- **Pythia-12B multi-seed pilot.** First mixed-data SAE seed on Pythia-12B at Pythia-6.9B-matched hyperparameters (multiplier 4, L1 5e-4, layer 18). Output: `results/dark_subspace/generated/sae_dark_subspace/p12b_mixed_sae_seed47/`. Gate at recon cosine $\geq 0.95$ before scheduling additional seeds for a second multi-seed anchor. Subsequently expanded to a five-seed cohort (seeds 47-51).
- **GPT-Neo multiplier-8 pilot.** Mixed-data SAE on GPT-Neo-2.7B at multiplier 8, L1 5e-4 (versus the failed multiplier-4 baseline). Gate at recon cosine $\geq 0.95$.

### PR-8. Statistical Reporting

- AUROC point estimates with paired bootstrap 95% confidence intervals across $n_{\mathrm{boot}}=10000$ resamples.
- Holm-Bonferroni correction across cross-model causal cells.
- Decisive criteria stated above.
- Cross-seed standard deviation reported wherever multi-seed evidence is available (~~Pythia-6.9B N=6~~ Pythia-6.9B N=5 per Amendment 2026-05-06; Pythia-12B N=5).
- Per-text scores produced by the paper scripts; not bundled in this artefact (each carries SAE checkpoint references and large array fields).

## Verification protocol

To verify any numerical claim in the paper:

1. Identify the paper passage and its source JSON path from the README "Claim-Source Map".
2. Read the corresponding JSON file under `results/dark_subspace/`.
3. Compare the paper number against the on-disk number to within the verifier's $\pm 0.002$ default tolerance (some asserts use $\pm 0.001$ where rounding admits tighter checks).

The verifier `python3 scripts/dark_subspace/verify_claims.py` automates steps 1-3 across all paper-cited cells; a passing run reports `Asserted check summary: N/N pass, 0 fail`.

### Amendment 2026-05-06 — PR-2 multi-seed cohort harmonisation (Pythia-6.9B N=6 to N=5)

- **Date.** 2026-05-06.
- **Original cohort (struck through above).** Pythia-6.9B is the multi-seed causal anchor with N=6 seeds (42_postfix, 43, 44, 45, 46, 47). Cross-seed standard deviation reported at N=6.
- **New cohort.** Pythia-6.9B reported at N=5 seeds (42_postfix, 43, 44, 45, 46), drop seed 47. Cross-seed standard deviation reported at N=5.
- **Reason.** Reporting harmonisation to align all multi-seed Pythia rows in the cross-architecture table to a uniform N=5 SAE-init reporting basis, matching the Pythia-1B multi-seed cohort seed list. Pattern A (drop seed 47) was selected because seeds 42_postfix through 46 align one-to-one with the Pythia-1B N=5 seed list. The N=6 cohort is preserved on disk and bundled at `results/dark_subspace/paper_claims/p69_n6_complete.json`. Per-metric materiality is NEGLIGIBLE: all five canonical metrics shift by less than 0.005 between N=5 and N=6 cluster means. The verifier asserts the materiality verdict directly. The within-Pythia strong-drop anchor claim survives both reporting conventions unchanged.
- **Cross-reference.** Bundled JSONs `paper_claims/p69_n5_harmonized_2026-05-06.json` and `paper_claims/p69_n6_complete.json`. Subselection script `scripts/dark_subspace/p69_n5_harmonize.py`.

### Amendment 2026-05-02b — Post-hoc falsification audit (held-out partition-fit probe for $d_K$)

- **Date.** 2026-05-02.
- **Status.** **Post-hoc falsification audit, not a pre-registered item.** This amendment was drafted after the held-out partition-fit probe outcome was observed. It does not retroactively claim pre-registration for the protocol.
- **Audit protocol.** The $d_K$ direction is fit on a random 70 percent of the canonical labelled pool (1400 examples) and $\mathrm{score}_K$ is evaluated on the disjoint 30 percent held-out partition (600 examples), with 10 random splits (split seeds 0..9) and a 10000-resample paired bootstrap on the held-out subset.
- **Anchor selection rule.** Per-model anchor is the SAE seed with the highest reconstruction cosine in the per-model multi-seed cohort. Pythia-6.9B uses seed 43 (recon cosine 0.979); Pythia-12B uses seed 49 (recon cosine 0.992). The rule selects on SAE quality, not on the held-out reduction itself, and was fixed in advance of the held-out estimation.
- **Pass criterion (audit-defined).** Held-out drop greater than (in-partition mean drop minus 2 cross-split standard deviations).
- **Outcome.** Both anchors fail the audit-defined pass criterion. The qualitative ordering residual $\geq$ original $>$ reconstruction is preserved on the held-out set for both anchors. The audit reframes $d_K$ as a labelling-partition-tuned projection rather than an intrinsic membership direction. Reported in the manuscript Limitations and in `app:heldout_dk_protocol`.
- **Cross-reference.** `results/dark_subspace/paper_claims/heldout_dk.json`. Script `scripts/dark_subspace/heldout_dk_eval.py`.

### Amendment 2026-05-02 — PR-2 reconstruction cosine gate hierarchy

- **Date.** 2026-05-02.
- **Original gate (struck through above).** Mean reconstruction cosine $\geq 0.95$ on the member and non-member corpus.
- **New gate hierarchy.** STRICT gate at reconstruction cosine $\geq 0.90$. YELLOW permissive band at $[0.85, 0.90)$ admitted with disclosure for the directional sign-test cohort observation only. Reconstruction cosine values below the YELLOW band are admitted to the cohort observation with explicit below-permissive disclosure when the inversion observation does not require high reconstruction cosine, and are excluded from any analysis whose validity does require high reconstruction cosine.
- **Reason.** The amendment was empirically required after observation of the directional sign-test cohort. Three cohort rows fall below the original 0.95 threshold (Pythia-1B at 0.87 in the YELLOW band, Pythia-2.8B at 0.78 below YELLOW, Falcon-7B at 0.77 below YELLOW). The directional inversion observation is computed from the comparison between original and residual AUROC and does not require high reconstruction cosine to be informative. The Pythia-6.9B and Pythia-12B causal anchors used for the headline within-Pythia strong-drop claim sit at reconstruction cosine above 0.99 and would pass either the original 0.95 gate or the amended 0.90 strict gate. The amendment scope is therefore limited to admitting the three lower-recon-cosine rows to the cohort-wide observation with disclosure, and does not affect the headline causal-anchor claim.
